The TabFact dataset contains factual claims about entities paired with evidence tables.
Each row represents one claim. Refuted claims (is_factual=0) contain DELIBERATELY
incorrect values crafted to look plausible while contradicting the evidence; entailed
claims (is_factual=1) are factually correct. Every cell described below has ALREADY
been flagged as erroneous by an upstream detector; the question is whether the change
was a deliberate factual manipulation (intentional) or an accidental data artefact
(unintentional).

Known intent signals (deceptive manipulation = gain-targeted, in the sense of making a
false claim that still reads as plausible):

A claim author who wants a refuted (false) claim to still look credible chooses a
replacement value that is internally plausible but breaks the claim's link to the
correct evidence:
  - A flagged subject_type that is inconsistent with the claim's true domain (e.g. an
    Athlete or Team subject paired with claim_domain "Politics" or "Business" instead
    of "Sports") indicates the entity category was deliberately swapped to manufacture
    a false-but-plausible-sounding claim.
  - A flagged claim_domain that is inconsistent with subject_type for the same reason
    (e.g. a "Party" subject_type paired with a claim_domain other than "Politics").
  - A flagged value whose magnitude is inconsistent with its claim_domain (e.g. a
    Sports value far outside typical game-score/ranking magnitudes, or a Demographics
    value far outside typical population-count magnitudes) indicates the quantity was
    deliberately altered to misstate the claim while remaining a plausible-looking
    number.
  - A flagged subject_location that does not correspond to any real geographic place
    plausible for the subject indicates the location was deliberately altered to
    misstate the claim.

Known intent signals (no plausible motive / noise):

A flagged cell whose value is empty, null, or a numeric placeholder (e.g. 0 or a
blank string in subject_entity, or a non-positive value in value) serves no
plausible deception goal, since a credible false claim requires a plausible-looking
replacement, not an empty or degenerate one. Such values are more consistent with an
accidental data artefact than a deliberate claim manipulation. In this corpus, the
deception motive is expected to dominate, since every flagged cell originates from a
deliberate claim-refutation process; rules in this class exist mainly as a safety net
and may rarely or never fire.

Column descriptions and validity rules (for reference; use these together with the
intent signals above, not as a separate error-detection pass):

- subject_entity: The name of the primary entity the claim is about (a person,
  team, company, country, etc.). Must be a non-empty string. Cannot be blank,
  null, or a numeric placeholder.

- subject_type: The category of the subject entity. Must be exactly one of:
  Athlete, Team, Organization, Leader, Company, Executive, Candidate, Party.
  No other values are valid.

- subject_location: The geographic location associated with the subject entity
  (city, country, or region). Must be a non-empty string. A subject_entity
  that is a Team or Athlete should have a valid location that corresponds to
  a real geographic place.

- value: A numeric quantity referenced in the claim (e.g., a score, count,
  amount, or measurement). Must be a positive integer greater than 0.
  Values below 100 are implausible given the dataset scale. Values above
  500,000,000 are implausible for most domain-relevant statistics.

- claim_domain: The subject-matter domain of the claim. Must be exactly one of:
  Sports, Demographics, Business, Politics. No other values are valid. This is
  the field most directly targeted by the deceptive cross-domain swaps
  described above.

Cross-column rules:

- If subject_type is "Athlete" or "Team", claim_domain should be "Sports".
  Other combinations (e.g., Team in "Politics") are unusual and likely a
  deliberate cross-domain swap.

- If subject_type is "Leader", "Executive", or "Candidate", claim_domain
  should be "Politics" or "Business". A Leader or Candidate in "Sports" is
  implausible and likely a deliberate cross-domain swap.

- If subject_type is "Party", claim_domain must be "Politics".

- The value column must be consistent with the claim_domain: Sports values
  are typically game scores, counts, or rankings (rarely above 10,000,000);
  Demographics values can be large population counts (millions are plausible);
  Business values can be revenues or profits (billions are plausible);
  Politics values are typically vote counts, percentages, or seat counts.
