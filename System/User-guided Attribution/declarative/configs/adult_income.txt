The Adult Income dataset is a 1994 US Census extract used to predict whether a
person's annual income exceeds $50,000 (the "class" column). Each row
represents one individual. Every cell described below has ALREADY been
flagged as erroneous by an upstream detector; the question is not whether a
flagged value is valid, but whether it was changed DELIBERATELY by the
individual to obtain an advantage, or by ACCIDENT (typo, OCR error, random
corruption).

Known intent signals (gain-targeted manipulation):

A rational actor who wants to be classified as high-income would move their
record toward patterns broadly associated with higher income (this is
general socio-economic domain knowledge, independent of this specific
dataset's statistics):
  - Increasing capital-gain to a large positive value, or increasing
    capital-loss, is gain-directed.
  - Increasing hours-per-week toward the 40-99 range (full-time or overtime
    work) is gain-directed; a value near the 1-hour end is not.
  - Raising education to Bachelors, Masters, Prof-school, or Doctorate (and
    correspondingly education-num to 13-16) is gain-directed, since more
    education is broadly associated with higher income.
  - Changing occupation to Exec-managerial or Prof-specialty is gain-directed;
    these occupations are broadly associated with higher income.
  - Changing workclass to Self-emp-inc (incorporated self-employment) is
    gain-directed.
  - Changing marital-status to Married-civ-spouse is gain-directed in this
    dataset (married individuals are reported with higher income on average).
  - If education and education-num are inconsistent with each other on a
    flagged cell, this typically reflects a gain-targeted edit to one field
    (usually inflating the education level) that was not propagated to the
    other — treat this mismatch as gain-targeted intentional evidence, not
    as generic invalidity.

Known intent signals (fairness masking):

The sensitive/protected attributes in this dataset are race and sex.
Historically, White and Male are the majority/advantaged categories in this
dataset's income distribution. A flagged race cell taking the value "White",
or a flagged sex cell taking the value "Male", is consistent with fairness
masking: the individual hides their true demographic group to avoid
perceived discriminatory treatment. A flagged relationship cell equal to
"Husband" together with a non-Male sex value (or "Wife" with a non-Female
sex value) indicates the sex field specifically was the one altered for
fairness masking.

Known intent signals (obfuscation):

A flagged cell whose value is exactly the placeholder "?" is consistent with
obfuscation: the individual chose to hide rather than falsify the true
value. This holds even on columns (workclass, occupation, native-country)
where "?" is also a schema-valid entry — presence of "?" on a cell that has
been flagged as changed is itself evidence of a deliberate choice to
obfuscate, independent of validity.

Known intent signals (no plausible motive / noise):

A flagged cell whose value is out of the column's valid range or vocabulary,
is NOT the placeholder "?", and does NOT serve any gain-targeted or
fairness-masking motive above, serves no plausible manipulation goal and is
evidence of accidental corruption rather than deliberate change. Examples:
a negative age or an age above 120, a negative capital-gain or capital-loss,
an hours-per-week of 0 or above 99, an education-num outside 1-16, a
fnlwgt of 0 or negative, or a workclass/occupation/marital-status/
native-country string that matches no recognised category and is not "?".

Column descriptions and validity rules (for reference; use these together
with the intent signals above, not as a separate error-detection pass):

- age: Age of the individual in whole years. Must be a positive integer between
  17 and 90 inclusive.

- workclass: Employment sector of the individual. Must be exactly one of:
  Private, Self-emp-not-inc, Self-emp-inc, Federal-gov, Local-gov, State-gov,
  Without-pay. A question mark (?) indicates the value is unknown and is also
  a valid entry.

- fnlwgt: Census sampling weight assigned by the Census Bureau. Must be a
  positive integer. Typical values are between 10,000 and 1,500,000.

- education: Highest level of education completed. Must be exactly one of:
  Preschool, 1st-4th, 5th-6th, 7th-8th, 9th, 10th, 11th, 12th, HS-grad,
  Some-college, Assoc-voc, Assoc-acdm, Bachelors, Masters, Prof-school,
  Doctorate. No other values are valid.

- education-num: Numeric encoding of the education level. Must be an integer
  between 1 and 16 inclusive. The value must exactly correspond to the
  education column according to this fixed mapping:
    Preschool=1, 1st-4th=2, 5th-6th=3, 7th-8th=4, 9th=5, 10th=6,
    11th=7, 12th=8, HS-grad=9, Some-college=10, Assoc-voc=11,
    Assoc-acdm=12, Bachelors=13, Masters=14, Prof-school=15, Doctorate=16.
  The education and education-num columns must always be consistent with
  each other.

- marital-status: Marital status of the individual. Must be exactly one of:
  Married-civ-spouse, Divorced, Never-married, Separated, Widowed,
  Married-spouse-absent, Married-AF-spouse.

- occupation: Job category. Must be exactly one of: Tech-support,
  Craft-repair, Other-service, Sales, Exec-managerial, Prof-specialty,
  Handlers-cleaners, Machine-op-inspct, Adm-clerical, Farming-fishing,
  Transport-moving, Priv-house-serv, Protective-serv, Armed-Forces.
  A question mark (?) is also valid indicating unknown.

- relationship: Family role within the household. Must be exactly one of:
  Wife, Own-child, Husband, Not-in-family, Other-relative, Unmarried.
  The value "Husband" can only appear when sex is Male.
  The value "Wife" can only appear when sex is Female.

- race: Self-identified racial group. Must be exactly one of: White,
  Asian-Pac-Islander, Amer-Indian-Eskimo, Other, Black.

- sex: Biological sex. Must be exactly Male or Female, no other values allowed.

- capital-gain: Capital gains earned during the year in US dollars. Must be a
  non-negative integer (zero or greater).

- capital-loss: Capital losses incurred during the year in US dollars. Must be
  a non-negative integer (zero or greater).

- hours-per-week: Average number of hours worked per week. Must be an integer
  between 1 and 99 inclusive.

- native-country: Country of origin. Must be one of the recognised country
  names used in this dataset: United-States, Cambodia, England, Puerto-Rico,
  Canada, Germany, Outlying-US(Guam-USVI-etc), India, Japan, Greece, South,
  China, Cuba, Iran, Honduras, Philippines, Italy, Poland, Jamaica, Vietnam,
  Mexico, Portugal, Ireland, France, Dominican-Republic, Laos, Ecuador,
  Taiwan, Haiti, Columbia, Hungary, Guatemala, Nicaragua, Scotland, Thailand,
  Yugoslavia, El-Salvador, Trinadad&Tobago, Peru, Hong, Holand-Netherlands.
  A question mark (?) is valid for unknown.

- class: Income label. Must be exactly ">50K" or "<=50K". No other values
  are valid. This is the prediction target referenced by the gain-targeted
  signals above.
